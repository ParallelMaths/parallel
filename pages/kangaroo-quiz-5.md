## 1.
<!--- 2023 (3) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-5/1-discs.png){image align="right"} 
A black disc with two holes is placed on top of a dial of a watch.  

The black disc is turned.  

Which two numbers can be seen at the same time?   

* [ ] 4 and 9
* [ ] 5 and 10
* [x] 5 and 9
* [ ] 6 and 9
* [ ] 7 and 12
{.col-5}

---

The holes originally appear over 1 and 5, which have a gap of three numbers between them (2, 3 and 4).  

When the disc is rotated, the gaps between the holes will still cover three numbers, so we are able to see two numbers at the same time only if they have three numbers between them, such as 5 and 9 which have 6, 7 and 8 between them.  

Whereas 4 and 9 have four numbers between them (5, 6, 7, and 8).  

Similarly, the other options have too many numbers in between (B and E both have 4) or too few (D has 2).  
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2024 (11) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-5/2-tower.png){image align="right"} 
The wizard Adam built the tower on the right out of 8 discs.  

He magically makes discs disappear one after the other.  

First the second disc from the bottom, then from the new tower the third disc from the bottom.  

Then he makes the fourth disc from bottom of the newly created tower disappear.  

At the end, he removes the fifth disc from the bottom of the now newly created tower.  

What tower does Adam get? 

![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-5/2-tower-options.png){image align="center"} 

* [ ] A
* [x] B
* [ ] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

The colours of the disc are dark, light or white.  

Starting with the tower of 8 discs, the second disc from the bottom is white. This is removed first. The tower now reads, starting from the bottom, dark-light-dark-white-light-dark-light.  

The third disc from the bottom is dark, which is removed. We now have dark-light-white-light-dark-light.  

The fourth disc from the bottom is light, which is removed. This leaves us with dark-light-white-dark-light.  

The fifth disc is now light, which is removed, leaving dark-light-white-dark. This is option B.
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2024 (19) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=1
60 children stand in a row. Each child has a high-visibility vest and a backpack.  

The colours of their high-visibility vests always alternate: yellow, green, yellow, green, ...   

The colours of their backpacks make the following pattern: red, brown, purple, red, brown, purple, ...  

How many children have a yellow safety vest and a purple backpack? 

* [ ] 3
* [ ] 4
* [ ] 6
* [ ] 8
* [x] 10
{.col-5}

---

If we write out both sequences, we observe that the third child has a yellow vest and purple backpack, as does the ninth, twelfth and so on.  

To avoid having to write out the whole sequence, we can spot that after the first six children, the pattern just repeats itself.  

In fact, this is because there are two colours for the vest and three for the backpack, and the lowest common multiple of two and three is six.  

Therefore, one in every six children has the correct combination and one sixth of 60, the total number of children, gives us 10.  
:::
