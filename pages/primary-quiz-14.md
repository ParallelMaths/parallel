## 2.
<!--- 2011 (8) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
Andrew writes the letters from the word KANGAROO in the fields of a table.  

He can begin where he wants and then must write each consecutive letter in a field that shares at least one point with the previous field.  

Which of the following tables could Andrew not produce?  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-14/2-kangaroo.png){image align="center"} 

* [ ] A
* [ ] B
* [ ] C
* [x] D
* [ ] E

---

In table D, if we start from the field containing K in the top left, this field shares a side (and therefore at least one point) with the field containing A, immediately to its right.  

From here, we can move to N (diagonally left and down), which shares one point with A.  

Next, we can continue to G, immediately to the right of N.  

At this point we become stuck. The only remaining fields that share at least one point with G are the two fields each containing the letter O, but G and O are not consecutive letters in the word ‘KANGAROO’.  
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2013 (12) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=2
Johann stacks 1 × 1 cubes on the squares of a 4 × 4 grid.  

The diagram shows how many cubes were piled on top of each other on each square of the grid.  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-14/3-stacks.png){image align="center"} 

What will Johann see if he looks from behind (hinten) at the tower?

![](/resources/primary-quiz-14/3-stacks-a.png){image align="center"} 

* [ ] A
* [ ] B
* [x] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

In each column, Johann will be able to see the tallest pile of cubes, even if it is not the pile closest to him.  

Viewing from behind (hinten), in the column furthest to the right, this has a height of 4 and this is the tallest pile in that column, so Johann will only be able to see this first tower of 4 cubes.  

Moving left, the tallest pile in the second and third columns is 3, whereas in the furthest left column the tallest pile has 2 cubes.  

Note that in the third column, Johann can see over the first pile of 2 cubes so that the tallest pile he sees is 3 cubes high, even though it is in the middle of the tower.  

We are then looking for a view with the number of cubes being 4-3-3-2, moving from right to left. This is option C.  
:::


## 4.
<!--- 2011 (17) --->
::: problem id=4_1 marks=2
If you are given the three corner points of a triangle and want to add a fourth point to make the four corners of a parallelogram.  

In how many places can the fourth point be placed?  

* [ ] 1
* [ ] 2
* [x] 3
* [ ] 4
* [ ] Depends on the triangle
{.col-5}

---

To produce a parallelogram, the triangle must be duplicated, rotated 180 degrees and then attached to a side of the original triangle such that the side lengths match. Since a triangle has 3 sides, this may be done in 3 ways.  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-14/4-triangles-a.png){image align="center"} 

Alternatively, we can draw a line parallel to each side of the triangle passing through the point opposite the chosen side of the triangle.  

Since there are 3 sides to a triangle, this produces 3 lines which meet in 3 places.  

These meeting points are where we can add a point to form a parallelogram.

:::


## 5.
<!--- 2011 (21) --->
::: problem id=5_1 marks=3
A cat had 7 kittens. The kittens had the colours white, black, ginger, black-white, ginger-white, ginger-black, and ginger-black-white.  

In how many ways can you choose four cats so that any two of the chosen four have a colour in common?  

* [ ] 1
* [ ] 3
* [x] 4
* [ ] 6
* [ ] 7
{.col-5}

---

If we pick the white kitten, then for the other three we can only pick kittens that also contain the colour white, these being black-white, ginger-white and ginger-black-white.  

If we pick the black kitten, then for the other three we can only pick kittens that also contain the colour black, these being black-white, ginger-black and ginger-black-white.  

If we pick the ginger kitten, then for the other three we can only pick kittens that also contain the colour ginger, these being ginger-white, ginger-black and ginger-black-white.  

The other possibility is that we pick all the double-coloured kittens and the triple-coloured kitten.  

This makes 4 ways in total.  
:::
