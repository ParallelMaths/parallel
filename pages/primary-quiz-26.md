## 1.
<!--- 2016 (1) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
Which of the following road signs has the most axes of symmetry? 

![](/resources/primary-quiz-26/1-signs.png){image align="center"} 

* [ ] A
* [ ] B
* [x] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

B and D have no axes of symmetry.  

A has one horizontal axis of symmetry, through the centre of the sign.  

E has one vertical axis of symmetry, through the centre of the sign.  

C has two axes of symmetry, one vertical and horizontal, both through the centre of the sign.
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2016 (8) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/primary-quiz-26/2-beds.png){image align="right"} 
Four girls are sleeping in a room with their heads on the grey pillows.  

Bea and Pia are sleeping on the left hand side of the room with their faces towards each other; Mary and Karen are on the right hand side with their backs towards each other.  

How many girls sleep with their right ear on the pillow?  

* [ ] 0
* [ ] 1
* [x] 2
* [ ] 3
* [ ] 4
{.col-5}

---

On the left of the room, Bea and Pia are facing each other and so the girl in the bottom left bed has her left ear on the pillow and girl in the top left bed has her right ear on the pillow.  

On the right of the room, Mary and Karen are facing in opposite directions, therefore the girl in the top right bed has her right ear on the pillow and the girl in the bottom right bed has her left ear on the pillow.  

This makes for 2 girls in total with their right ear on the pillow.
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2012 (12) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=2
Which three puzzle pieces do you need to complete the large puzzle? 

![](/resources/primary-quiz-26/3-puzzles.png){image align="center"} 

* [ ] 1,3,4
* [ ] 1,3,6
* [ ] 2,3,5
* [x] 2,3,6
* [ ] 2,5,6
{.col-5}

---
![](/resources/primary-quiz-26/3-puzzles-a.png){image align="right"} 
Piece 2 is the only piece that could fit in the centre of the puzzle, since we need a hole in the bottom of the piece and a tab sticking out of the top.  

Then, piece 3 must be to the right of piece 2, since we need a tab pointing to the left, to connect with the hole in the right of piece 2, as well as a straight side on the right.  

Finally, a corner piece must be positioned in the top right and only piece 6 has a hole in the bottom that can connect with the tab on the top of piece 3.
:::


## 4.
<!--- 2016 (17) --->
::: problem id=4_1 marks=2
Tim, Tom and Jim are triplets. Their brother Carl is exactly 3 years younger.  

All four are having their birthdays today.  

How old can the four brothers be altogether? 

* [x] 53
* [ ] 54
* [ ] 56
* [ ] 59
* [ ] 60
{.col-5}

---

If all four brothers were the same age, then the sum of their ages would be a multiple of 4.  

However, since Carl is 3 years younger, the actual sum of their ages will be 3 less than a multiple of 4.  

The only possibility is then 53.
:::


## 5.
<!--- 2018 (20) --->
::: problem id=5_1 marks=3
The five balls weigh 30 g, 50 g, 50 g, 50 g and 80 g.  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-26/5-balls.png){image align="center"} 

Which of the balls weighs 30 g? 

* [ ] A
* [ ] B
* [x] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

The sum of the weights of all the balls is 260 g. The third scale is balanced, so there must be 130 g on each side.  

The only possibility for this is 80 + 50 on the left and 50 + 50 + 30 on the right.  

Therefore, A and D are either 80 or 50, and B, C and E are either 50 or 30.  

From the first scales we know that A + B < C + D.  If D weighs 50 g, then C + D is at most 50 + 50 = 100 g. But then A must weigh 80 g and so A + B is at least 80 + 30 = 110 g. This does not agree with the scales.  

This means that D must weigh 80 g and that A must weigh 50 g.  

Looking at the second scales, E + B > A + C. But A weighs 50 g and B, C and E are all either 50 or 30.  

The only possibility is 50 + 50 = 100 g on the left and then 50 + 30 = 80 g on the right, meaning ball C weighs 30 g.  
:::
