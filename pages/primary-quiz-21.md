## 1.
<!--- 2012 (5) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
On the 24th February 2012 Grandfathers chicks hatched.  
 
There are 29 days in February in 2012.  

How old are the chicks today on 15th March 2012? 

* [ ] 29 days
* [ ] 24 days
* [ ] 22 days
* [x] 20 days
* [ ] 15 days
{.col-5}

---

The chicks are 0 days old on the 24th February.  

There are 29 – 24 = 5 days until the 29th February and then 15 days from then until the 15th March.  

This means that the chicks will be 5 + 15 = 20 days old on the 15th March.
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2014 (10) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
Look at the following pattern:  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-21/2-pattern.png){image align="center"} 

Which square has to replace the question mark so that the white area and the black area are equally big?  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-21/2-patterns.png){image align="center"} 

* [ ] A
* [x] B
* [ ] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

By reflecting the triangles in the diagonals of each square, we can see that every white triangle can be paired with a black triangle of equal area.  

This is true except for the square in the far-right column of the middle row which is entirely white.  

To ensure that the white and black areas are equally big, we need to pair this entirely white square with an entirely black square, as seen in option (B).  
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2012 (15) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=2
The figure here has a perimeter of 42 cm. 

![](/resources/primary-quiz-21/3-shapes.png){image align="center"} 

The figure was made from eight equally sized squares.  

What is the area of the figure?  

* [ ] 8 cm<sup>2</sup>
* [ ] 9 cm<sup>2</sup>
* [ ] 24 cm<sup>2</sup>
* [x] 72 cm<sup>2</sup>
* [ ] 128 cm<sup>2</sup>
{.col-5}

---

The perimeter is made up of 14 equally sized lengths of a square, so each square side is of length 42 ÷ 14 = 3.  

The area is then `8 × 3^2 = 72` <sup>2</sup>.
:::


## 4.
<!--- 2015 (15) --->
::: problem id=4_1 marks=2
Lucy and her mother were both born in January.  

On 23rd March 2015 Lucy adds together her year of birth, that of her mother, her age, and that of her mother.  

Which answer does she get?  

* [ ] 4028
* [ ] 4029
* [x] 4030
* [ ] 4031
* [ ] 4032
{.col-5}

---

Lucy’s year of birth is given by the current year, 2015, minus her age. Similarly, her mother’s year of birth is given by 2015 minus her mother’s age.  

When we add these together, along with the age of Lucy and that of her mother, the ages all cancel out, leaving 2015 + 2015 = 4030.  

Algebraically, let L be the age of Lucy and M be the age of her mother.  

Then, the sum is (2015 – L) + (2015 – M) + L + M = 2015 + 2015 + (L – L) + (M – M) = 4030. 
:::


## 5.
<!--- 2017 (20) --->
::: problem id=5_1 marks=3
![](/resources/primary-quiz-21/5-floor.png){image align="right"} 
A square floor is made up of triangular and square tiles in grey and white. 

What is the smallest number of grey tiles that have to be swapped with white tiles, so that the floor looks the same from all four given viewing directions?

* [ ] three triangles, one square 
* [ ] one triangle, three squares 
* [x] one triangle, one square 
* [ ] three triangles, three squares 
* [ ] three triangles, two squares

---

![](/resources/primary-quiz-21/5-floor-answer.png){image align="right"} 
We move the middle triangle on the bottom side to the bottom right side (shown in green).  

We also take the middle square closest to the right-hand side and move it one square diagonally upwards to the right (shown in red).  

This means that the pattern on each side is one triangle with one square vertically above it on the left-hand side.  
:::
