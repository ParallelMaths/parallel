## 1.
<!--- 2022 (3) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-3/1-grid.png){image align="right"} 
One of the five coins A, B, C, D or E shall be placed in an empty square so that there are exactly two coins in each row and in each column.  

Which coin should be moved?   

* [ ] A
* [ ] B
* [x] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

First, we count the number of coins in each row and column, observing that the first column on the left and the bottom row both contain three coins.  

Therefore, since we can only move one coin, we must choose a coin that is in both this first row and the last row, namely C.  

We can place it where there is currently only one coin in that row or column, in this case we place C in the second column and fourth row, immediately to the right of B (not that the question asks us where C must be placed!).
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2023 (11) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-3/2-cars.png){image align="right"} 
The diagram shows four cars 1, 2, 3 and 4.  

The arrows show where the cars move to in 5 seconds.  

Which cars will crash into each other?

* [ ] 1 and 2
* [ ] 1 and 3
* [ ] 1 and 4
* [x] 2 and 3
* [ ] 3 and 4
{.col-5}

---

Cars 1 and 4 will not crash together since their paths run parallel and so will never intersect.  

Cars 2 and 3 will crash together since they are pointing directly towards each other along the same diagonal.  

Car 4 will have moved out of the way of cars 2 and 3 by the time they crash.  

Car 1 will not reach the position of car 3 until after car 3 has passed this point.
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2024 (14) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-3/3-caterpillar.png){image align="right"} 

Lucas has these five pieces of a puzzle available.  

He wants to lay a caterpillar consisting of a head, a tail and 1, 2 or 3 parts in between.  

How many different caterpillars can Lucas build? 

* [ ] 3
* [x] 4
* [ ] 5
* [ ] 6
* [ ] 7
{.col-5}

---

The head must go first and the tail last, leaving the three middle parts in some order to be determined.  

If there is one piece in the middle, then this can only be the cherry piece, since this has a rectangular connection on the right which can connect with the tail piece whilst having a round connection on the left to fit in with the head piece.  

If there are two pieces in the middle, then we have two options for the second piece (the one connected to the head) since the connection head is an oval shape, that is either the cherry or the grape piece could be second.  

If the cherry piece is second, then the apple piece must be next, since it can fit with the rectangular connection of cherry on the left and that of the tail piece on the right.  

If the grape piece is second, then the sequence must be head-grape-cherry-tail, with the connections being round, round, round and then rectangular.  

Finally, if there are three parts in the middle, then the only possibility is head-grape-cherry-apple-tail, with the connections being round-round-rectangular-rectangular-rectangular.  

This totals 4 possibilities.  
:::
