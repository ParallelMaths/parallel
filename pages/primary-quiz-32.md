## 2.
<!--- 2017 (10) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
Only four players score goals in a handball game.  

Each one scored a different number of goals.  

Michael scored the fewest number of goals.  

If the other players altogether managed to score 20 goals in total, what is the maximum number of goals Michael could have scored? 

* [ ] 2
* [ ] 3
* [x] 4
* [ ] 5
* [ ] 6
{.col-5}

---

Since the other players scored 20 altogether, we need to find three different numbers that sum to 20.  

However, we also want to make the smallest of these numbers as large as possible, since Michael scored less than this and we are trying to find the maximum number of goals that he scored.  

The possibility for the other three players with the lowest number as large as possible is that they scored 5, 7 and 8 goals, respectively.  

We cannot increase the lowest score and maintain the total sum of 20 without also reducing the score of 7 or 8, which would then cause two scores to be the same which is not possible.  

Since Michael scored the fewest, he scored less than 5, and so his maximum score is 4 goals.  
:::



## 3.
<!--- 2017 (12) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=1
Tom strings together the numbers from 1 to 20 and obtains the 31-digit number 1234567891011121314151617181920.  

Then he deletes 24 digits of the number, so that the remaining number is as big as possible.  

Which number does he obtain?  

* [ ] 9671819
* [ ] 9567892
* [x] 9781920
* [ ] 9912345
* [ ] 9818192
{.col-5}

---

The remaining number must have 31 – 24 = 7 digits.  

To make it as big as possible, we want the largest digits possible in the columns further to the left.  

First, we delete the first eight digits in the original number to leave the ninth digit, 9, in the millions’ column of the new number.  

The largest digit that we can now place next is the twenty-fifth digit in the original number, 7, and then for the next column we choose the twenty-seventh digit, 8. We delete the digits of 1 in between.  

The last four digits in our new number are then the same as in the original number, namely 1, 9, 2 and 0 to form the number 9781920.  
:::


## 4.
<!--- 2018 (16) --->
::: problem id=4_1 marks=2
From a list with the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, Monika chooses 3 different numbers whose sum is 8.  

From the same list Daniel chooses 3 different numbers whose sum is 7.  

How many of the numbers were chosen by both Monika and Daniel? 

* [ ] none
* [ ] 1
* [x] 2
* [ ] 3
* [ ] It cannot be determined.
{.col-5}

---

The only group of three that sums to 7 is 1, 2 and 4, so Daniel must have chosen these three numbers.  

However, from the same list there are two possible groups of three that sum to 8, so Monika could have picked 1, 2 and 5 or 1, 3 and 4.  

If Monika picks the first triplet, she has two numbers in common with Daniel: 1 and 2.  

If Monika picks the second triplet, she has two numbers in common with Daniel: 1 and 3.  

Therefore, regardless of which triplet Monika chooses, she will have two numbers in common with Daniel.  
:::

Which of the pictures does not show the box? 

* [ ] A
* [x] B

---

If we take the two black squares as the front face, as in option A, then the top of the box is formed from two grey squares, the back has one grey square and one white, the base has one black square and one grey, and the sides are both white squares.  

Starting from picture A, rotating it 90 degrees, so that the fully black side moving to the top, produces picture D.  

From here, rotating another 90 degrees gives picture C, and 90 degrees more yields picture E.  

Picture B is impossible. It is not possible to form a side with two white squares from the given cardboard.
