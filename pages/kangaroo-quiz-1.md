# Kangaroo Quiz 1

* Answer all of the questions in 3 minutes - if you're unsure of the answer, make a guess before you hit submit. 
* No calculators allowed.


## 1.
<!--- 2024 (Ecolier) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
Lizzy has 7 coins of a kind.  

She buys three fruits at the market. Each fruit has a different price.  

How much is the most expensive fruit?

* [ ] 2 coins
* [ ] 3 coins
* [x] 4 coins
* [ ] 5 coins
* [ ] 6 coins
{.col-5}

---

To maximise the price of the most expensive fruit, we require other two fruits to be as cheap as possible.  

Since all fruits different price, the other two must cost 1 and 2 coins.  

The most expensive is therefore 7 – 1 – 2 = 4 coins.  
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2024 (Ecolier) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-1/2-laps.png){image align="right"} 
Max draws a large circle in the sand. Lisa adds the letters A, B, C and D.  

Lisa and Max start at the same time from point A. Lisa goes clockwise around the circle, Max runs anti-clockwise around the circle (see picture).  

They meet for the first time at point B, then at C, at D and then again at point A.  

How many times has Max run around the circle by then? 

* [ ] 1-times 
* [ ] 2-times
* [x] 3-times
* [ ] 4-times
* [ ] 5-times
{.col-5}

---

In the time that Max runs `3/4` of a lap, Lisa runs `1/4`.  

So Max is 3 times faster than Lisa.  

They meet each time Lisa has covered `1/4` of a lap, during which time Max has covered another `3/4` of a lap.  

Max runs `3/4` of a lap every time they meet, for a total of `3/4 × 4 = 3` laps.
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2024 (Ecolier) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-1/3-penguin.png){image align="right"} 
Penguin Peter goes fishing every day and brings 9 fish for his two children.  

Every day he gives 5 fish to the first child he sees. The other child then gets 4 fish.  

In the last few days, a child has had a total of 26 fish.  

How many fish did the other child get?

* [ ] 19
* [ ] 22
* [ ] 25
* [x] 28
* [ ] 31
{.col-5}

---

By trial and error (or using Algebra) we can see that the child with 26 fish got 5 fish on 2 days and 4 fish on 4 days. There is no other combination of 4s and 5s that will give 26.  

So there have been 6 days altogether and a total of `6 × 9 = 54` fish have been handed out.  

The second child therefore received `56 − 26 = 28` fish.
:::
