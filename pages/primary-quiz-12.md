## 1.
<!--- 2013 (5) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
Anna, Bob and Chris are altogether 31 years old.  

How old will all three be altogether in three years time? 

* [ ] 32
* [ ] 34
* [ ] 35
* [ ] 37
* [x] 40
{.col-5}

---

After 3 years, each person’s age will have increased by 3, so in total the increase in age is 3 × 3 = 9 years, giving the answer of 31 + 9 = 40.  
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2011 (6) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/primary-quiz-13/2-water.png){image align="center"} 
1000 litres of water is passed through the water system as shown, into two identical tanks.  

At each junction the water separates into two equal amounts.  

How many litres of water end up in Tank Y?  

* [ ] 800
* [x] 750
* [ ] 666.67
* [ ] 660
* [ ] 500
{.col-5}

---

The 1000 litres splits into 500 litres at the first junction.  

Each amount of 500 litres separates again into 250 litres at the second junction.  

Into tank Y, the pipe on the far right inputs 250 litres.  

The middle pipe receives 250 litres each side from above, so this pipe inputs 500 litres into tank Y.  

Tank Y receives a total of 250 + 500 = 750 litres.  
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2013 (10) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=2
Maria drew the following figures on square sheets of paper. 

![](/resources/primary-quiz-12/3-figure.png){image align="center"} 

How many of these figures have the same perimeter as the square sheet of paper itself? 

* [ ] 2
* [ ] 3
* [x] 4
* [ ] 5
* [ ] 6
{.col-5}

---

In the first shape, consider the right-hand vertical side. Imagine pushing this to the right until it reaches the side of the piece of paper.  

By doing this, we can see that the lengths of the vertical sides of the shape add up to the same value as the vertical side lengths of the piece of paper.  

Similarly, we can do the same with the horizontal sides of the first shape, and with some of the other shapes. In this way we can see that the first, fourth, fifth and sixth shapes will have the same perimeter as the piece of paper.  

One way of thinking about this is that the corners of the paper have been ‘folded in’ to make the new shape.  

Whereas, in the second and third shapes, if we add up the side lengths, we can see that they will sum to the perimeter of the piece of paper plus the additional vertical side lengths drawn on the inside of the piece of paper.  
:::


## 4.
<!--- 2011 (15) --->
::: problem id=4_1 marks=2
Paul wanted to multiply a whole number by 301, but forgot to include the zero and multiplied by 31 instead.  

His answer was 372.  

What should his answer have been?  

* [ ] 3,010
* [x] 3,612
* [ ] 3,702
* [ ] 3,720
* [ ] 30,720
{.col-5}

---

We perform 372 ÷ 31 = 12 to find the original number.  

We can now multiply 12 by 301 to obtain 3612.
:::


## 5.
<!--- 2014 (20) --->
::: problem id=5_1 marks=3
There are 5 songs on a playlist: Song A lasts 3 mins, song B lasts 2 mins 30s, song C lasts 2 mins, song D lasts 1 min 30s, and song E lasts 4 mins.  

These 5 songs are played non-stop one after the other.  

Song C has just started playing when Andy leaves the house. Exactly one hour later he returns.  

Which song is playing when Andy came back?   

* [x] A
* [ ] B
* [ ] C
* [ ] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

The total length of the playlist is 3 + 2.5 + 2 + 1.5 + 4 = 13 mins.  

Since 4 × 13 = 52 mins, after this amount of time the playlist will have completely played through 4 times and song C will have started playing again.  

After 2 mins more, song D will start playing (52 + 2 = 54 mins after Andy left).  

1.5 mins after that, song E will start (54 + 1.5 = 55.5 mins after Andy left).  

4 mins later, song A will begin (55.5 + 4 = 59.5 mins after Andy left).  

When Andy comes back after 60 mins, song A will still be playing.  
:::
