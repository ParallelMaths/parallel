## 1.
<!--- 2023 (1) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-4/1-candles.png){image align="right"} 
Five children each light a candle at the same time.  

Lisa blows out the candles at different times.  

Now they look as shown in the picture.  

Which candle did Lisa blow out first? 

* [ ] A
* [ ] B
* [ ] C
* [x] D
* [ ] E
{.col-5}

---

The longer a candle is lit, the more the wax will be burnt down and so the shorter it will become.  

The candle blown out first will have burnt down the least and so will be the tallest, which is D.
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2024 (4) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-4/2-circles.png){image align="right"} 
Lisa writes the numbers 1, 2, 4, 5 and 6 in the circles of the pattern.  

She writes each of the five numbers exactly once, and if she adds up the numbers along one of the three straight lines, she gets 11 each time.  

Which number did she write in the circle with the question mark?  

* [ ] 1
* [ ] 2
* [x] 4
* [ ] 5
* [ ] 6
{.col-5}

---

Firstly, note that the image has a vertical line of symmetry through the circle with a question mark.  

So, we may swap the two upper numbers or the bottom two and obtain the same result.

Note that 5 and 6 are the only two numbers that can sum to 11 from just a pair, i.e. 5 + 6.  

The other numbers are two small and so will require at least three numbers for the sum to reach 11. Therefore, we may place 5 and 6 in the bottom positions where the straight line summing to 11 only consists of two numbers.

Next, observe that adding 1 and 2 to 6 and 5 obtains 7.  

This would leave the remaining number, in the question mark position, as 11 - 7 = 4.
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2011 (11) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=1
![](/resources/kangaroo-quiz-4/3-worms.png){image align="right"} 
Mia has 3 cards with three-digit numbers on them.  

If she adds the three numbers together, she gets the number 782.  

Unfortunately, a worm has eaten one digit of each card.  

What number does she get when she adds up the three digits that the worm has eaten?

* [ ] 8
* [ ] 9
* [x] 10
* [ ] 11
* [ ] 12
{.col-5}

---

Summing the final digits, we have that 3, 4 and some missing digit sum to 2.  

The only possibility is that the missing digit is 5, so that 3 + 4 + 5 = 12.  

Whilst this gives 2 in the units’ column, note that we also have to carry 1 into the tens’ column.  

Summing up the tens’ column, we have that two missing digits, the 1 from 412 and our carried 1 all add up to 8.  

There can be no carries from the tens’ column, since in the hundreds’ column 2, 1 and 4 add to give 7 exactly.  

Any carry from the tens’ to the hundreds’ would increase the 7 digit in 782.  

Therefore, the two missing values in the tens’ columns sum to 8, and with the missing units’ digit of 2 found earlier, we have the answer of 10.  
:::
