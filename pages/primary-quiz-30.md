## 1.
<!--- 2017 (2) --->
::: problem id=1_1 marks=1
A fly has 6 legs, a spider 8.   

Therefore 3 flies and 2 spiders together have the same amount of legs as 9 chickens and...

* [ ] 2 cats
* [ ] 3 cats
* [x] 4 cats
* [ ] 5 cats
* [ ] 6 cats
{.col-5}

---

Altogether, the flies and spiders have 3 × 6 + 2 × 8 = 18 + 16 legs.  

The chickens have 9 × 2 = 18 legs.  

This means that the cats must have 16 legs altogether.  

Therefore, we have 16 ÷ 4 = 4 cats.
:::


## 2.
<!--- 2017 (9) --->
::: problem id=2_1 marks=1
![](/resources/primary-quiz-30/2-square.png){image align="right"} 
A rectangle is twice as long as wide.  

Which fraction of the rectangle is coloured in grey? 

* [ ] `1/4`
* [x] `3/8`
* [ ] `3/4`
* [ ] `1/2`
* [ ] `3/5`
{.col-5}

---

![](/resources/primary-quiz-30/2-square-a.png){image align="right"} 
By connecting the midpoints of the sides of the rectangle, along the lines of symmetry, we can split the rectangle into 8 identical right-angled triangles.  

We can now see that 3 out of the 8 triangles are coloured in grey, therefore the required fraction is `3/8`.  

Alternatively, if we take the width of the rectangle as 1, then its length is 2, since it is twice as long as it is wide. Its area is then `1 × 2 = 2`.  

The height of each triangle is `1/2` and the base is 1. The shaded area is then `3 × 1 × 1/2 × 1/2 = 3/4.`  

The fraction that is shaded is that `3/4 ÷ 2 = 3/8`.  
:::


## 3.
<!--- 2017 (11) --->
::: problem id=3_1 marks=2
A furniture shop sells 3-seater, 2-seater and 1-seater sofas that each have an equally wide armrest on the left and the right hand side.  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-30/3-sofas.png){image align="center"} 

Each seat is equally wide (see picture).  

Together with the armrests the 3-seater sofa is 220 cm and the 2-seater sofa 160 cm wide.  

How wide is the 1-seater sofa? 

* [ ] 60 cm
* [ ] 80 cm
* [ ] 90 cm
* [x] 100 cm
* [ ] 120 cm
{.col-5}

---

The total widths of the 3-seater and 2-seater sofas differ by the width of one seat.  

Therefore, one seat has a width equal to this difference, that is 220 – 160 = 60 cm.  

This means that the width of both armrests is given by the total width of the 3-seater minus the width of three seats, which is 220 – 3 × 60 = 40 cm.  

This agrees with the fact that the width of both armrests should be equal to the total width of the 2-seater minus the width of two seats, which is 160 – 2 × 60 = 40 cm.  

Finally, the width of the 1-seater sofa is 60 + 40 = 100 cm.
:::


## 4.
<!--- 2017 (16) --->
::: problem id=4_1 marks=2
There are eight kangaroos in a row, as seen in the picture.  

![](/resources/primary-quiz-30/4-kangaroos.png){image align="center"} 

Two kangaroos, that are standing next to each other and that are looking into each others eyes, are changing places by hopping past each other.  

This is carried out until no more jumps are possible.  

How often did a change of places occur? 

* [ ] 2
* [ ] 10
* [ ] 12
* [x] 13
* [ ] 16
{.col-5}

---

Initially, there are 5 kangaroos facing the right and 3 facing the left.

Those facing the left start in positions 4, 7 and 8, counting from the left.

The kangaroo that starts in position 4 must hop past 3 kangaroos until it is in the far-left position, no longer looking into the eyes of another kangaroo.

The kangaroo that starts in position 7 must then hop past 5 kangaroos until it is in position 2, immediately to the right of the first kangaroo that is facing the left.

The kangaroo that starts in position 8 must finally hop past 5 kangaroos until it is in position 3, immediately to the right of the second kangaroo facing the left.

![](/resources/primary-quiz-30/4-kangaroos-a.png){image align="center"} 

This totals 3 + 5 + 5 = 13 changes of places.
:::


## 5.
<!--- 2019 (21) --->
::: problem id=5_1 marks=3
Emil likes taking pictures of his 8 cousins.  

Each one of Emil's 8 cousins are on two or three of the pictures.  

There are exactly 5 of Emil's cousins in each of the pictures.  

How many pictures does Emil take?  

* [ ] 3
* [x] 4
* [ ] 5
* [ ] 6
* [ ] 7
{.col-5}

---

Since there are 5 cousins per picture, the total number of cousins in all pictures must be a multiple of 5 (with all cousins appearing multiple times).  

If all cousins appeared twice, then there would be 2 × 8 = 16 cousins seen altogether across all the pictures.  

If all cousins appeared thrice, then there would be 3 × 8 = 24 cousins seen altogether across all the pictures.  

However, some cousins appear two times and other three. Therefore, we are looking for a multiple of 5 between 16 and 24.  

The only possibility is that 20 cousins are seen across all pictures. Hence, 20 ÷ 5 = 4 selfies were taken.  

Indeed, the solution is that 4 cousins appear in two pictures and the other 4 appear in three pictures.
:::
